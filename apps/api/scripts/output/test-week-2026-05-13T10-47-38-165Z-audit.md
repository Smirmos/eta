## Significant issues

1. **C/AE2 main-set duration (150 min) is below Friel's IM AE-portion prescription, but the rationale claims it's "at the low end of the 3–4 h range".** KB `03-workouts.md` line 302 (Appendix C, p. 455 verbatim): "Ironman athletes should do an AE portion of 3 to 4 hours (besides warm-up and cooldown)." The Sunday C/AE2 workout has warmup 900s + main 9000s + cooldown 900s = 10,800s total (3.0 h), but the AE *main set* itself is 9,000s = 150 min = **2.5 h** — 30 min short of Friel's stated 3-hour minimum. The rationale (weeklyDetail.workouts[5].rationale) reads "3 hours sits at the low end of Ironman AE prescription (3-4 hours)," conflating total session duration with AE-portion duration. KB is explicit: the 3–4 h range is "besides warm-up and cooldown," so the AE portion would need to be 180 min (not 150) to sit at the low end. This is a coaching-quality issue (the longest workout of the week is meaningfully shorter than the KB prescription it claims to honor) and a rationale-vs-JSON contradiction per audit-rule 6 above. Acceptable mitigation note: macroPlan wk 15 globalNotes / deviations explicitly capped weekly hours at 9.5 h with the rationale that this is week 1 of a compressed ramp from a 9 h baseline — so a sub-3 h long ride is *defensible* against the macro cap, but the workout-level rationale should say so rather than mis-state the KB range.

2. **B/AE2 rationale invokes a "long-session day exemption" on a non-long-session-day.** athleteProfile.longSessionDays = `['fri','sun']` (line 67 of test-profile.json) — Saturday is **not** a long-session day. The B/AE2 workout is placed on Saturday (2026-05-16) with totalDurationSeconds = 4500 s (75 min). The rationale (weeklyDetail.workouts[4].rationale, final sentence) reads: "Long-session day exemption is why this runs 75min vs your 90-min weekday cap." Two factual problems: (a) Saturday is not in longSessionDays, so the exemption doesn't apply on this day, and (b) 75 min is under the 90 min cap anyway, so no exemption is needed. The placement itself is fine — the macro keySession explicitly specified `dayOfWeek: "sat"` for the B/AE2 limiter swim — but the rationale's justification is factually wrong and contradicts the JSON (`longSessionDays` field). Per audit-rule 6 this is a Significant finding.

## Minor issues

The two sport-specific workouts should be separated by two days, such as run on Monday and Thursday, swim on Tuesday and Friday, and bike on Wednesday and Saturday, with Sunday a day off." The plan has D/AE1 (run, Thu, 40 min, z1 recovery) immediately followed by D/AE2 (run, Fri, 105 min, z2 long). The Thursday run is explicitly framed as recovery in its rationale ("this is the lowest-stress session of the week by design"), and Friel's rule 20 (p. 229) carves out active-recovery sessions for two-a-day ordering purposes — but rule 20 is about same-day two-a-days, not about same-sport day-spacing across the week. Strict reading of rule 17 → 1-day same-sport gap is sub-spec. Defensible because: (a) Thu D/AE1 is z1-only and labeled "recovery, day after the bike threshold test" — its KB role per line 549 is "duration or TSS for this workout should be the lowest in a given week," which the plan honors (40 min, lowest TSS of the 6 at 39.5); (b) Tue is occupied by B/T2 swim and Wed by C/T1 bike, so the only weekday slots left for a 6th workout are Mon (rest) or Thu, and Thursday-recovery before Friday-long aligns with rule 7 ("recovery before/after breakthrough", p. 210, cited verbatim in `04-weekly-templates.md` line 456). Flag as Minor with the recommendation that the rationale explicitly cite rule 7 / rule 20's recovery-session carve-out rather than leave the reader to infer it.
